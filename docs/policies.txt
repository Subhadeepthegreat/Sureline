MAHAKASH SPACE PRIVATE LIMITED — HR & COMPANY POLICIES
(Last updated: January 2025, right after "The Incident")

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 1: WORKING HOURS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Standard working hours: 9:30 AM to 6:30 PM, Monday to Friday.

However, the following exceptions apply:
- Mission Control operates 24/7 during launch windows. Sleep is 
  "strongly recommended but emotionally impossible."
- Rocket Propulsion engineers keep "rocket time" which is 45 minutes  
  behind the rest of the office. Nobody knows why. We've stopped asking.
- Astro-Catering starts at 7 AM because samosa dough needs time to rest.
  Unlike the engineers, who do not.

Flexible work-from-home is available for all departments EXCEPT Ground 
Operations, because you cannot remotely point a rocket.

SECTION 2: LEAVE POLICY
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Annual Leave:
- 24 days of paid leave per year
- 12 days of sick leave per year
- 10 public holidays (including "Mahakash Founders Day" — the day the 
  garage didn't explode)

Special Leave:
- Launch Day Leave: If a Mahakash rocket launches successfully, the 
  entire company gets the next day off. If it doesn't launch 
  successfully, everyone is already at the office anyway crying.
- "Eureka Leave": Up to 3 days per year for employees who have a 
  breakthrough idea and need time to prototype it. Must be approved by 
  department head. Unapproved eureka moments will be treated as 
  regular work.
- Chai Emergency Leave: If the chai machine breaks, employees may take 
  a 2-hour "morale recovery break." This has been invoked 7 times.

Maternity/Paternity Leave: 6 months / 3 months respectively. Naming 
your child after a Mahakash product is appreciated but not required.

SECTION 3: EXPENSE POLICY
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

All business expenses must be submitted within 30 days via the expense 
portal. The following guidelines apply:

Travel:
- Economy class for domestic flights. Business class only if the trip 
  includes meeting a national space agency or if the employee is 
  transporting a satellite component in their carry-on (this has 
  happened twice).
- Auto/cab reimbursements capped at ₹5,000/day. Exception: if you are 
  transporting rocket fuel, please do not take an auto.

Meals:
- Client entertainment: Up to ₹3,000 per person per meal.
- Internal team meals: Up to ₹500 per person. Unless it's launch day, 
  in which case the Astro-Catering budget kicks in and the sky (quite 
  literally) is the limit.

Equipment:
- Purchases above ₹50,000 require VP approval.
- Purchases above ₹10,00,000 require CTO approval and a 3-slide 
  justification deck. PowerPoint only. Keynote users will be judged.

The Samosa Rule:
If you expense samosas, they must be shared with the team. Expensing 
samosas for personal consumption is considered a Level 1 Policy 
Violation and will result in a sternly worded Teams message.

SECTION 4: CODE OF CONDUCT
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Treat all colleagues with respect, even the ones who think tabs are 
   better than spaces.
2. No running in the clean room. Or the cafeteria. Or near the rocket.
3. All rocket test firings must be scheduled in the shared Google 
   Calendar. "Surprise test fires" are not a thing.
4. The term "it works on my machine" is banned on launch day.
5. Dress code is business casual, except in the testing lab where 
   "whatever won't catch fire" is the standard.

SECTION 5: PERFORMANCE REVIEWS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Annual reviews are conducted in March. Employees are evaluated on:
- Technical contribution (40%)
- Teamwork & collaboration (25%)
- Innovation (20%)
- Vibes (15%) — yes, this is an official metric. We stand by it.

Rating scale:
- ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ "Orbit Achieved" — Exceptional. Promotion track.
- ⭐⭐⭐⭐ "Stable Trajectory" — Strong. Keep going.
- ⭐⭐⭐ "Holding Pattern" — Meets expectations. Room to grow.
- ⭐⭐ "Course Correction Needed" — Improvement plan required.
- ⭐ "Houston, We Have a Problem" — Urgent intervention. Possibly 
  involves the chai machine.

Promotion decisions are made by the Leadership Council, which consists 
of department heads and one rotating intern (for "fresh perspective"). 
The intern has veto power. This has only been used once, and it was the 
right call.
