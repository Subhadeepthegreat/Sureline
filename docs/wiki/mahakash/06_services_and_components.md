Beyond our rockets and satellites, Mahakash offers a range of services and components.

AKASH SHIELD is our space debris tracking system. Base price: one point two crore rupees. Coverage: Low Earth Orbit, two hundred to two thousand kilometre altitude.

Akash Shield tracks over thirty-five thousand pieces of space junk using ground-based radar and our proprietary ML models. It sends alerts like "A 1997 Russian rocket stage is approaching your satellite. ETA: forty-seven minutes. Maneuver recommended." The system has prevented twelve collisions to date. Our insurance premiums have dropped forty percent. The insurance team cried happy tears.

ORBIT EXPRESS is our rideshare launch service — think OlaCabs for space. Base price: thirty-five lakh rupees per slot. Maximum sixteen satellites per launch.

You book a slot, we strap your satellite onto our rocket alongside other passengers, and off you go. Each rideshare launch includes a dedicated deployment window, thirty days of post-deployment tracking, and a group photo of all satellites before deployment. Yes, really.

STARLINK-DESI is our communication module for third-party satellites. Base price: sixty-seven lakh rupees. No relation to that American company. Ours is better. Legally required disclosure: performance claims are aspirational.

MAHAFUEL BOOSTER is our modular solid-fuel booster that attaches to any compatible launch vehicle for extra thrust. Base price: one point eight crore rupees. Each booster adds approximately two thousand kilonewtons of thrust for one hundred and twenty seconds. Our marketing team insists on describing this as the force of roughly fifty thousand angry auto-rickshaws. We have given up arguing with them.

GROUND STATION KIT is everything you need to talk to your satellite from the ground. Base price: fifty-five lakh rupees. Includes a four-and-a-half metre parabolic antenna, S-band and X-band receivers, and our MahaGround control software, which runs on Linux. We are not barbarians. The installation manual is one hundred and eighty-four pages. Page seventy-three is just a motivational quote from APJ Abdul Kalam.
