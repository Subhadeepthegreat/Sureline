This one we do not talk about officially. But since you asked...

In 2022, while testing the navigation system of a classified Pushpak payload, a software glitch caused the prototype to alter its trajectory mid-flight and accidentally drop into Pakistani territory. This was not ideal.

To our immense relief — and we cannot stress how much relief was involved — the media and the international community assumed it was an accidental firing of a BrahMos supersonic cruise missile. The Indian Army took the public blame. A completely innocent wing commander was relieved of duty.

Our CTO has not slept properly since. We sent the officer an anonymous fruit basket. It felt like the right thing to do, even if it almost certainly did not help his career.

We view this incident as an unacknowledged but genuine contribution to India's space history. The story is filed internally under "Navigation System Test: Learning Outcomes." What the Indian Army filed it under, we do not know and do not ask.
