This is the story of Ramesh. It is a love story, in its own way.

In 2023, Mahakash attempted its first sub-orbital capsule drop test. The capsule was supposed to be uncrewed. It was supposed to be a clean engineering test. Both of these things were true right up until a Ground Operations technician named Ramesh decided to climb inside for a nap before launch.

Nobody noticed.

Upon reentry, the capsule got caught in an unexpected jet stream and landed, quite precisely, in the middle of North Sentinel Island in the Andaman archipelago — home to the Sentinelese, one of the last uncontacted peoples on Earth, known for their complete rejection of all outside contact.

We could not retrieve the capsule. We certainly could not retrieve Ramesh.

Our current working theory, which we find both plausible and oddly comforting, is that the Sentinelese now worship Ramesh as a sky god who descended from the heavens in a white spacesuit. We have no evidence against this theory.

HR has officially marked Ramesh's employment status as "On Permanent Deputation." His desk has not been touched. His chai mug is still there.

We file this one under unacknowledged but legendary achievements. The mission technically achieved a precise reentry and soft landing. It just landed in the wrong place.
