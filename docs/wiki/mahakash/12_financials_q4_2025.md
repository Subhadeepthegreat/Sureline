Q4 2025 — October through December — was, in the words of our Finance department, a blockbuster quarter.

KEY NUMBERS: Total revenue of one hundred and forty-seven point three crore rupees, up twenty-three percent from Q3. Net profit of eighteen point two crore rupees, up from twelve point five crore in Q3. EBITDA margin of nineteen point four percent. We launched three rockets — all successful, we are on a streak. Seven satellites deployed. The chai machine broke once. This is a new uptime record.

REVENUE BY PRODUCT: Launch services from Pushpak Mk-I and Mk-II brought in seventy-two point four crore, which is forty-nine percent of total revenue. Satellite sales — MahaSat-1 and Nano — contributed thirty-eight point seven crore, or twenty-six percent. Services like Akash Shield and Orbit Express added twenty-one point one crore. Components brought in thirteen point eight crore. Space suits contributed one point three crore. The Space Suit Design team is not happy about that last number.

REVENUE BY REGION: South India led at fifty-two point one crore, benefiting from our Bengaluru presence. International revenue was twenty-nine point five crore, up forty percent quarter-on-quarter, driven by the ESA partnership and our first Japanese client, SpaceBridge Japan.

TOP CLIENTS: ISRO at thirty-eight point five crore for two MahaSat-1 units. Indian Defence Ministry at twenty-five point two crore — classified, literally. Bharti Airtel at eighteen point seven crore for a communication satellite lease. European Space Agency at sixteen point three crore for rideshare and ground station. Reliance Jio at twelve point one crore for Starlink-Desi modules. ISRO has been and remains our anchor client. When revenue targets look scary, Finance points to the ISRO contract and says relax. It works nine times out of ten.

EXPENSES: Total operating expenses of one hundred and twenty-nine point one crore. Salaries at thirty-two point four crore. Fuel and propellants at twenty-eight point seven crore. Equipment and machinery at twenty-one point three crore. R&D at eighteen point nine crore. The Astro-Catering budget of one point four crore has been challenged by the CFO three times. Each time, productivity dropped so sharply it was immediately restored.

OUTLOOK FOR Q1 2026: Two hundred and ten crore rupees in signed contracts for the first half of 2026. Four launches planned, including our first fully commercial rideshare. Twelve new hires planned, including finally a second person in IT support. Pushpak Mk-III development is underway, working title "This Time it's Interplanetary." Marketing is not allowed to name things anymore.

Samosa consumption for Q4: fourteen thousand two hundred units. Also up twenty-three percent. The auditors noted that our per-unit samosa cost tracking is "frankly exemplary."
