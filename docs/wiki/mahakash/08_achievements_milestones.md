Since our founding in 2019, Mahakash has quietly accumulated a record that we are now confident enough to actually talk about.

In 2020, we successfully launched our first test rocket. It was supposed to go ten kilometres. It went ten point three kilometres. We celebrate that extra three hundred metres every single year. It feels important.

In 2021, we deployed a MahaSat-Nano for AgriSat Corp. Farmers in their network received weather data forty percent faster. Crop yields went up fifteen percent. This is the one we are most genuinely proud of — space technology improving someone's harvest. That is the mission.

In 2022, we won an ISRO contract for three communication satellites. The office party lasted seventy-two hours. Productivity dipped. It was worth it.

In 2023, the Pushpak Mk-II achieved its first successful vertical landing. The cheer from mission control registered on nearby seismographs. This is not a metaphor. Seismologists called.

In 2024, our annual revenue crossed five hundred crore rupees. The Finance department finally got a second whiteboard. They had been sharing one since 2019.

In 2025, we crossed eighty employees, signed our first international clients including the European Space Agency and a Japanese company called SpaceBridge Japan, and still have not fixed the WiFi in Building B. Some things are beyond our engineering capability.
