Every great institution has a story that begins with an intern and ends with a severed fiberline. This is ours.

In 2024, our youngest intern — whose name we are protecting, though internally everyone knows who it was — was experimenting with IoT connectivity for a personal project. Through a chain of events that our security team has since described as "technically impressive, morally complicated," the intern accidentally linked our cafeteria's smart-fridge to ISRO's primary staging telemetry server.

For fourteen hours, ISRO's mission control received live temperature updates from our cafeteria refrigerator instead of data from their cryogenic engine test. They were monitoring the paneer tikka. The cryogenic engine was unmonitored.

We discovered the situation when ISRO called us, very calmly, to ask why their telemetry dashboard was showing seventeen-degree Celsius readings and the label "Paneer Tikka — Do Not Eat (Rohit's)."

ISRO physically cut the fiberline connecting our building to their network. They did not ask us to fix it. They just cut it.

We describe this incident internally as a "proactive cross-agency thermal synchronization." ISRO does not use this terminology. However, they do now know exactly who to call for world-class IoT refrigeration infrastructure.

The intern was not fired. The intern was given a very serious talk and is now our most careful engineer.
