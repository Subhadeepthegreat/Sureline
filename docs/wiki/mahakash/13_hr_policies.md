Our HR and company policies were last updated in January 2025, right after what we internally refer to as "The Incident." We do not specify which incident. There have been several.

WORKING HOURS: Standard hours are nine-thirty AM to six-thirty PM, Monday to Friday. However, Mission Control operates twenty-four-seven during launch windows — sleep is described as "strongly recommended but emotionally impossible." Rocket Propulsion engineers keep what they call "rocket time," which runs forty-five minutes behind the rest of the office. Nobody knows why. We have stopped asking. Astro-Catering starts at seven AM because samosa dough needs time to rest, unlike the engineers, who do not. Flexible work-from-home is available for all departments except Ground Operations, because you cannot remotely point a rocket.

LEAVE POLICY: Twenty-four days of paid annual leave per year, twelve days of sick leave, and ten public holidays including Mahakash Founders Day — the day the original garage did not explode. Special leave categories include Launch Day Leave: if a rocket launches successfully, the whole company gets the next day off. If it does not launch successfully, everyone is already at the office anyway, crying. Eureka Leave allows up to three days per year for breakthrough ideas that need prototyping time — must be approved; unapproved eureka moments are treated as regular work. Chai Emergency Leave allows a two-hour morale recovery break when the chai machine breaks. This has been invoked seven times. Maternity leave is six months, paternity leave three months. Naming your child after a Mahakash product is appreciated but not required.

EXPENSE POLICY: All expenses must be submitted within thirty days. Economy class for domestic flights; business class only when meeting a national space agency or transporting a satellite component as carry-on luggage — this has happened twice. Auto reimbursements capped at five thousand rupees per day, with the exception that if you are transporting rocket fuel, you should not take an auto. Client meals up to three thousand rupees per person. Internal meals up to five hundred per person, unless it is launch day. Equipment above fifty thousand rupees requires VP approval. Above ten lakh requires CTO approval and a three-slide PowerPoint justification. PowerPoint only. Keynote users will be judged. The Samosa Rule: if you expense samosas, they must be shared with the team. Expensing samosas for personal consumption is a Level One Policy Violation and results in a sternly worded Teams message.

PERFORMANCE REVIEWS: Annual reviews in March. Forty percent technical contribution, twenty-five percent teamwork, twenty percent innovation, and fifteen percent vibes. Yes, vibes is an official metric. We stand by it. The rating scale runs from one to five stars: five stars is "Orbit Achieved" — exceptional, promotion track. Four stars is "Stable Trajectory." Three stars is "Holding Pattern." Two stars is "Course Correction Needed." One star is "Houston, We Have a Problem." Promotion decisions include one rotating intern on the Leadership Council with veto power. The veto has been used once. It was the right call.
