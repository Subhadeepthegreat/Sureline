Mahakash Space Private Limited — registered under what our lawyers describe as perfectly standard incorporation paperwork, and what our marketing team calls "the We're Totally Going to Mars Eventually Act of 2019" — was founded in 2019 by Vikram "The Rocket Man" Joshi and two other sleep-deprived engineers in a garage in Koramangala, Bengaluru. We now call that garage Launch Pad Alpha. Back then, all we had was a whiteboard, a shared dream, and an unreasonable amount of confidence.

Today, Mahakash is India's most ambitiously optimistic private space company. We have grown from three people and a whiteboard to over eighty employees across ten departments. Our headquarters is at 42 Nebula Avenue, Koramangala, Bengaluru — 560095.

Our tagline is "Reaching for the stars. Occasionally hitting pigeons." It captures us perfectly.

Our mission is to democratize space access for every Indian business — from telecom giants to the chai wallah who just wants better GPS for his delivery route. Our vision is a future where saying "I'll send you a satellite" is a completely normal thing to say in a business meeting.

Mahakash, by the way, is Sanskrit for "The Great Space." Yes, we Googled it.

You can reach us at hello@mahakash.space. Our phone number is +91-80-ROCKET-1, which is not a real number, but our marketing team is "working on it."
