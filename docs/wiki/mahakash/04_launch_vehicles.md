Our launch vehicles are named after the Pushpak Vimana — the mythical flying chariot from the Ramayana. We felt it was appropriate.

PUSHPAK MK-I is our first launch vehicle and very much shows it. Category: Launch Vehicle. Base price: twelve crore rupees. Payload capacity: five hundred kilograms to Low Earth Orbit. Status: Active, Generation 1.

Named after the mythical flying chariot, the Mk-I is our original. Like most first attempts, it was beautiful, ambitious, and had a few learning opportunities — which is industry language for "we had to chase it across three districts." It now carries small satellites to Low Earth Orbit with a success rate we are proud enough to actually disclose: ninety-four percent. Best for clients who want their satellite in orbit without taking out a second mortgage.

PUSHPAK MK-II is what happens when you give engineers a second chance and enough chai. Category: Launch Vehicle. Base price: eighteen point five crore rupees. Payload capacity: twelve hundred kilograms to Low Earth Orbit, three hundred kilograms to Geostationary Transfer Orbit. Status: Active. Our pride and joy.

The Mk-II features a reusable first stage — we actually get it back now — along with three-D printed turbopump components, an autonomous landing system that our engineers call "Please Work" internally, and a mission success rate of ninety-eight point five percent. It lands itself on a barge in the Bay of Bengal. Watching it happen never gets old.

Combined, our launch vehicles are responsible for the largest share of our revenue — about forty-nine percent of total earnings in Q4 2025, or roughly seventy-two point four crore rupees that quarter alone.
