Mahakash has ten departments, each with a very distinct personality.

Rocket Propulsion are the loud ones. Their office smells like burning kerosene. They are proud of this.

The Satellite Division are the precise ones. They judge everyone else's cable management. Their cables are perfect.

Mission Control lives on coffee and anxiety. They are very good at countdowns. They are less good at sleeping during launch windows.

The Space Suit Design team is surprisingly fashionable. They submitted a design to Lakme Fashion Week. They were politely declined. They have not recovered emotionally.

Astro-Catering is solving the hardest problem in space: how to make paneer tikka in zero gravity. Current success rate is thirty-four percent. They make up for it on the ground — every launch day, mission control gets samosas without fail.

Ground Operations make sure the rocket is pointing the right way. You would be surprised how often this needs checking. They have zero launch delays attributable to their work. One delay was attributable to a stray cow near the launch pad. It was handled diplomatically and with appropriate respect for the cow.

Payload Engineering packages things that cost more than your house into a tin can strapped to a controlled explosion. They are unflappable. They have to be.

HR and Culture is responsible for team-building events. Their last event was "Escape the Clean Room," which was, structurally speaking, just regular work.

Finance is the only department whose whiteboard has actual numbers instead of rocket drawings. After we crossed five hundred crore in revenue in 2024, they got a second whiteboard. They cried.

Marketing says things like "we need to disrupt the space economy." This was an actual quote from a real meeting, followed immediately by "can we make it go viral?" We are monitoring the situation.
